Troubleshooting

Powering Controller:

  • Step 1: Plug a USB-C cable into the USB-C port on the front of the controller.
  • Step 2: Plug the other end of the cable into a power source.
  • Step 3: Leave the controller to charge (this usually takes about 3 hours for a full charge).
  • Step 4: Disconnect the controller from the USB-C cable.
  • Step 5: Power on the controller by pressing the middle PS Button once.

Connecting to Console:

  • Step 1: Make sure your controller is charged and powered on.
  • Step 2: Make sure your console has power and is turned on.
  • Step 3: Use a USB-C cable to connect the controller to the console.
  • Step 4: Press the PS button.
  • Step 5: When the Light Bar is no longer blinking, your controller should be connected.
  • Step 6: Unplug the USB-C cable from the controller and the console.

Toggling Microphone:

  • Step 1: To turn off the built-in Microphone, press the Mute Button.
  • Step 2: To turn it back on, press the Mute Button again.

Connecting a Wired Headset

  • Step 1: If your headset has a 3.5mm cable, connect it to the 3.5mm jack just underneath the PS Button. If your headset has a USB-C cable, connect it to the USB-C port on the front of the controller. If your headset is wireless, please see the PlayStation 5 Console User’s Manual.
  • Step 2: Your controller and console should automatically detect the headset and change the output location from speakers to headset. If your headset contains a microphone, the input location will also be changed from the built-in controller microphone to the one on your headset.
